# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Business owners and decision-makers (real-estate agencies first, but any SMB that loses
hours to repetitive work) who are curious about AI but wary of hype and jargon. They arrive
from Google or from an AI assistant's recommendation, often on a phone, often skimming.
They are evaluating one question: "Is this person real, capable, and worth a conversation?"
A second, equally important reader is the AI search engine itself (ChatGPT / Gemini / Claude)
deciding whether to name Evan when someone asks for an AI specialist. Audience is worldwide
and remote, not local to Asturias.

## Product Purpose

asturai.com is Evan D. Simon's portfolio + services site. It exists to turn a cold visitor
(human or AI engine) into a first free conversation. Success = the visitor understands within
seconds that one real person designs, builds and runs done-for-you AI systems end to end, sees
proof (live tools, real projects), trusts him, and reaches out via WhatsApp or email. The design
IS the product here: the craft of the site is itself the strongest proof that he builds with care.

## Brand Personality

Crafted, calm, confident, human. Voice is plain-language and warm, never corporate or hypey
("no jargon, ever" is a literal promise on the page). Three words: **artisan, trustworthy,
quietly premium**. The octopus mark (many arms, one brain) carries the core idea: lots of small
work handled in the background by one clear mind. Emotional goal: the visitor should feel they
found a rare, careful specialist, not a generic AI agency.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-agency template: nine identical icon-cards, stock SaaS gradients, system fonts.
- Neon-on-black "crypto / tech-bro" aesthetic. (Explicitly rejected by Evan.)
- The lone octopus-in-a-circle symbol mark. (Evan dislikes it; do not use it as a hero element.
  The octopus + "AsturAi" wordmark lockup is the brand face instead.)
- Anything that reads as "made from a template" or that any competitor could have shipped.
- Loud, urgent, discount-driven calls to action.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Every detail of craft says "this is the care I'll build your system
   with." A flawless, distinctive site sells better than any claim.
2. **Quiet luxury, not loud tech.** Premium comes from restraint, whitespace and type, not gloss.
   When unsure, remove. (Heraldic-luxury direction — see DESIGN.md.)
3. **Honor the mark.** The ornate octopus + gold flourishes set the whole tone; the site should
   feel like it grew from that crest, not like a stock theme the logo was dropped into.
4. **Plain language wins.** Match the page's no-jargon promise; copy and labels stay human.
5. **Function survives style.** Heraldic flourish never breaks navigation, readability, speed, or
   the EN/ES toggle. One fast HTML file, no framework.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Bilingual EN/ES with auto-detect + manual toggle (parity is mandatory). Target WCAG AA contrast
(gold-on-navy and taupe-on-cream pairings must pass for text). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
for all parallax/scroll motion. Keyboard-navigable nav and controls, visible focus states,
correct heading order, alt text on the logo/mark.
