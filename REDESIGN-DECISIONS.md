## ROUND 1 — Foundation (LOCKED ✅)

**The site has two jobs at once:**
- **Portfolio** — so a company thinking of hiring Evan (freelance / subcontractor for *their* clients) can see his work, demos, and what he's built, and judge if he's the right fit.
- **Pitch** — so a direct client can see what he could build *for them*.

**The name:** **AsturAi** — capital **A** at the start, capital **A** in the middle, **final i always lowercase** (so nobody reads it as an "L"). Meaning: Astur + the AI idea.

**The octopus mascot — keep it.** Works on two levels:
- *AI automation* — each tentacle acts on its own, but one brain runs them all (many arms, one mind).
- *Asturias* — the octopus (pulpo) is native to the northern coast of Spain; that's the root of the name.

**What to avoid:** the generic AI-startup look, and especially the tired "scroll down to see more" pattern. The moment someone scrolls down, he's lost. Hold the visitor on one "page"; moving through the site should feel like going **deeper inside it** (entering the next chapter/room), not scrolling down.

**Positioning (studio voice — never "I" / never "one person", to protect pricing):**
> AsturAi builds your entire AI system around how your business actually works — bespoke,
> start to finish, and kept running for you. Not off-the-shelf automation, not an agency
> that hands you off — a studio that designs it, builds it, **and stays with you.**

- Edge = **bespoke, personal, run-for-you**. Opposite of cold "factory automation."
- Spoken as **"we" (a studio)**, never "I'm the only one" — that would shrink the brand and undercut pricing.
- Reality (not shown on site): Evan builds the engine, his wife designs the website; could grow to 2–3 people.

**Visual direction / feeling:** intentionally undecided. We start from Evan's **sketch** and figure out the direction together.

## DESKTOP MECHANICS (LOCKED ✅)

- **One page, no scrolling down** to reach menus.
- **Scroll swaps the whole screen** to the next section/chapter — not a long page moving down or sideways.
- **Two signature transitions:**
  - *Effect 1:* something moves behind, something new overlays on top.
  - *Effect 2:* a fly-through — the visitor is taken into the center of the page to see the next thing.
- **Desktop-first.** Get it solid before anything else.
- **Mobile:** on hold completely for now; addressed later.
