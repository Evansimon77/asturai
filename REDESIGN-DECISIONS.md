## ROUND 1 — Foundation (LOCKED ✅)

**The site has two jobs at once:**
- **Portfolio** — so a company thinking of hiring Evan (freelance / subcontractor for *their* clients) can see his work, demos, and what he's built, and judge if he's the right fit.
- **Pitch** — so a direct client can see what he could build *for them*.

**The name:** **AsturAi** — capital **A** at the start, capital **A** in the middle, **final i always lowercase** (so nobody reads it as an "L"). Meaning: Astur + the AI idea.

**The octopus mascot — keep it.** Works on two levels:
- *AI automation* — each tentacle acts on its own, but one brain runs them all (many arms, one mind).
- *Asturias* — the octopus (pulpo) is native to the northern coast of Spain; that's the root of the name.

**What to avoid:** the generic AI-startup look, and especially the tired "scroll down to see more" pattern. The moment someone scrolls down, he's lost. Hold the visitor on one "page"; moving through the site should feel like going **deeper inside it** (entering the next chapter/room), not scrolling down.

**Positioning (studio voice — never "I" / never "one person", to protect pricing):**
> AsturAi builds your entire AI system around how your business actually works — bespoke,
> start to finish, and kept running for you. Not off-the-shelf automation, not an agency
> that hands you off — a studio that designs it, builds it, **and stays with you.**

- Edge = **bespoke, personal, run-for-you**. Opposite of cold "factory automation."
- Spoken as **"we" (a studio)**, never "I'm the only one" — that would shrink the brand and undercut pricing.
- Reality (not shown on site): Evan builds the engine, his wife designs the website; could grow to 2–3 people.

**Visual direction / feeling:** intentionally undecided. We start from Evan's **sketch** and figure out the direction together.

## DESKTOP MECHANICS (LOCKED ✅)

- **One page, no scrolling down** to reach menus.
- **Scroll swaps the whole screen** to the next section/chapter — not a long page moving down or sideways.
- **Two signature transitions:**
  - *Effect 1:* something moves behind, something new overlays on top.
  - *Effect 2:* a fly-through — the visitor is taken into the center of the page to see the next thing.
- **Desktop-first.** Get it solid before anything else.
- **Mobile:** on hold completely for now; addressed later.

## ROUND 2 — The Plan: Positioning & Offer (LOCKED ✅)

**1. Who we really beat.**
A *new field*. Most businesses just **live with the pain** — nothing bespoke existed. At
best they use a **general SaaS CRM** (requests, scheduling, calendar), never something built
for *their* company or people. Competitors offering AI automation will appear soon, but Evan
expects them to be **general / one-size-fits-all**. Our edge = **bespoke vs. generic.**

**2. The real result a client gets** (proof points, said without "AI/automation" jargon):
- **Hours back.**
- Staff money **stops being wasted on mundane work** — people do their real jobs smoothly
  (maybe fewer staff needed, but the point is wasted-money, not layoffs).
- **Fewer mistakes, more secure, privacy-law compliant** — see privacy below.
- **Everything about a client in one place** (one single source per client) — pull up a client
  instantly instead of hunting scattered places.
- **No lost leads** — every lead followed through; better lead-to-follow-up ratio. Especially
  for real estate paying a lot monthly (Idealista etc.) and losing leads to disorganization.

**Privacy & data protection (a core value, built into the code):**
- Each employee's clients are **private to them** by default.
- Shared only **on request**; the **boss / authorized people** can always access.
- **Compliant with EU + Spanish privacy authorities (EDPB, EDPS, AEPD)** — "protected by law,
  by design." This is its own proof point on the page.

**3. The entry offer — a FREE FIRST LOOK (not "first conversation free", not "light version"):**
A genuine, useful **diagnosis** at no cost. Two branches:
- **Website** → run the **AI Visibility Audit**: where they stand, how they compare to top
  competitors, how AI search engines see them (or don't), what competitors have that they lack.
- **Company** → analyze the whole operation and, **like a doctor**, recommend fixes — specific
  problems or a full system remake.
Structure: **free first look (diagnosis) → full assessment (paid) → bespoke build → monthly partnership.**

**4. Pricing (structure):** built **like a custom-made suit** — **no single price.**
- **One-time build:** priced per business type and scope (full system or one part).
- **Monthly fee:** support, improvements, add-ons, special features, upgrades to newest AI models.
- Internal range (NOT shown on site): ~€80/mo and up, scaling to ~€6,000/mo by company size.

**4b. Prices on the page → NO numbers (per marketing skill).**
- Don't show €80 (reads cheap, fights premium) or €6,000 (scares small businesses) or the range
  (turns us into a price tag to compare). Never lead with price — lead with value.
- Kill the cost-fear three other ways instead: (1) show value first so price feels small;
  (2) the free first look costs nothing — zero-risk "yes" to start; (3) one reassurance line that
  pricing is **shaped to each business and can start small and grow** — no figure named.
- The real euro number lands in the **personal conversation after the diagnosis**, set against
  the value found for *their* business (value-based pricing).
- Revisit a soft "starts small" line ONLY if we choose approachable over premium (parked → sketch).

**5. Premium vs. approachable feel** → still **parked** until the sketch.

**6. The one fear (two):** (a) **"Can they really do what they say?"** (is it real / will it work);
(b) **"How much will it cost me?"**. The site must **prove capability** and **calm cost-fear**
(via the three ways above) — without a scary number too early.
